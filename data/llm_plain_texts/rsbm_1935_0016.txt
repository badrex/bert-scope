tldr: 'John Rose Bradford was a prominent physician and physiologist who made significant contributions to British medicine and the biological sciences. He worked in various roles, including as a clinical teacher, researcher, and administrator, and was known for his influence on the growth of scientific thought in medicine. Bradford's research focused on the electric phenomena accompanying nervous activity and the spinal distribution of controlling nerves in organs. His work and dedication to medical education left a lasting impact on the medical field and future generations of students.'

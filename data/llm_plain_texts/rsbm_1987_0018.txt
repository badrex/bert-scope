tldr: This article is a biography of John Turton Randall, a notable scientist who made significant contributions in solid-state physics, radar technology, and molecular biology. He invented the cavity magnetron, which played a crucial role in World War II, and contributed to the discovery of the DNA double helix and the sliding filament mechanism of muscle contraction. Randall's success was due to his exceptional energy, foresight, and sound judgment, as well as his dedication to laboratory work.

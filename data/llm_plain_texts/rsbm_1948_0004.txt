tldr: Thomas Hastie Bryce was a prominent anatomist, archaeologist, and anthropologist who made significant contributions to the fields of embryology, anatomy, and Scottish archaeology. He served as a lecturer and professor at the University of Glasgow for 45 years and was a member of the Society of Antiquaries of Scotland. Bryce's research focused on the development of the spleen in Lepidosiren, early human ovum development, and the prehistory of Scotland, particularly the island of Arran.

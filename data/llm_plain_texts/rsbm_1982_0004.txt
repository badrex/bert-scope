tldr: The article is a biographical memoir of Alexander Edgar Douglas, a Canadian physicist known for his contributions to spectroscopy and his work during World War II. Born in Saskatchewan, Douglas went on to study physics at the University of Saskatchewan and later worked at the National Research Council of Canada. He made significant discoveries in the field of spectroscopy, including solving the mystery of unidentified interstellar absorption lines. Douglas also contributed to antisubmarine warfare efforts during World War II.

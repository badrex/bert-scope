tldr: This obituary honors Sir William Henry Power, a prominent public health officer who made significant contributions to the understanding of infectious diseases and their impact on public health. Power's work on the relationship between smallpox, diphtheria, and scarlet fever and milk consumption, as well as his investigations into lead poisoning and infant mortality, laid the foundation for modern public health practices. His dedication to scientific investigation and his intuitive understanding of the methods best suited for solving complex health problems made him a pioneer in the field.

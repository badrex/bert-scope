tldr: This article commemorates the life of Edmund Halley, a versatile scientist born in 1656. Halley made significant contributions to astronomy, mathematics, and geophysics, and was a close friend and supporter of Isaac Newton. He played a crucial role in the publication of Newton's Principia, providing financial support, assistance with calculations, and promoting Newton's work. Halley's other achievements include pioneering work in navigation, civil engineering, and the founding of population and actuarial statistics.

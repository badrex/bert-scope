tldr: This article discusses the challenges and future prospects of time-resolved macromolecular crystallography, particularly the Laue method. The author highlights the need for well-ordered crystals, rapid initiation of reactions, efficient data collection, and better ways to study solid-state kinetics. The article also emphasizes the importance of combining different methods and computational biochemistry to overcome limitations in time-resolved X-ray crystallography.

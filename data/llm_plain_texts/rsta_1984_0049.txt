tldr: This article discusses the evolution of fixed and mobile Earth stations for satellite communications over the past two decades, focusing on the technical, political, and operational factors that have influenced their design. The growth of satellite communication systems, such as Intelsat, has been remarkable, with millions of home television stations using high-powered satellites by the 1990s. The article explores the various types of Earth stations, their antenna sizes, and the different multiplex-modulation techniques used in satellite communication systems.

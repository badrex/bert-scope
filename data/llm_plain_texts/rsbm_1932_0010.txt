tldr: 'Sir Richard Threlfall was a renowned physicist and explosives expert who made significant contributions to the field of physics. Born in 1861, Threlfall attended Clifton College and later Caius College, Cambridge, where he excelled in his studies. He conducted groundbreaking experiments on explosives and was a skilled glass blower despite losing part of his fingers in an accident. Threlfall held the Professorship of Physics at the University of Sydney, where he established a state-of-the-art laboratory and mentored many students. He also played rugby and was an avid sportsman. Threlfall's life was filled with scientific achievements and adventures, making him a memorable figure in the history of science.'

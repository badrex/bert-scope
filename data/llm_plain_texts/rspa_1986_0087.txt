tldr: This article analyzes the accuracy and usefulness of economic predictions in macroeconomic policy-making. It examines the Treasury's forecasting record for GDP and inflation, discussing the size of errors, the deterioration of forecasts as the forecast horizon is extended, and any signs of improvement in methods. The author concludes that while there are challenges in economic modeling, predictions remain crucial for policy implementation.

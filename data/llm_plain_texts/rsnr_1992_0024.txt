tldr: This article provides new details about Benjamin Robins, a significant figure in 18th-century science and engineering. Robins made important contributions to the fields of ballistics, gunpowder, and calculus. He developed the ballistic pendulum for measuring musket-ball speed and studied air resistance and the pressures and temperatures of gunpowder gases. Robins also advocated for the use of rifling in English small arms and ordnance. Despite facing challenges and limited educational opportunities due to his background, Robins made a lasting impact on the scientific community of his time.

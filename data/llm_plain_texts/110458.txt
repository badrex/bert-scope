tldr: This article presents hygrometrical observations made on board His Majesty's surveying vessel Etna from March 27 to July 6, 1834. The observations include the height of the barometer, dew point, degrees of dryness and moisture, vapor elasticity, and vapor content. These measurements were taken at various times of the day and provide valuable data for understanding the weather and climate during that period.

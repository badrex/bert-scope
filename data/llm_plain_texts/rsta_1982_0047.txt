tldr: This article reviews recent advancements in atomic emission spectroscopy using an inductively coupled plasma (ICP) discharge source. The ICP discharge has become a popular source for emission spectroscopy due to its ability to analyze a wide range of elements and concentrations. The article discusses the characterization, diagnostics, and interpretation of the ICP emission spectrum, as well as the discharge and analyte properties in ICP spectrochemistry.

tldr: 'William Quarrier Kennedy was a renowned geologist and educator who made significant contributions to the fields of geology, tectonics, and African geology. He worked for the Geological Survey of Great Britain and later became the Chair of Geology at the University of Leeds, where he founded the Research Institute of African Geology. Kennedy was known for his ability to discern geological relations on a large scale and his exceptional memory, which helped him make major discoveries in Scotland and Africa.'

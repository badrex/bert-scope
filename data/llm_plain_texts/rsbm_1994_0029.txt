tldr: 'Vernon Ellis Cosslett was a prominent figure in electron microscopy, contributing to the fields of materials science, biology, and medicine. He founded the Electron Microscopy Section at Cambridge University's Cavendish Laboratory and played a significant role in uniting national and international efforts in electron microscopy after World War II. Cosslett's scientific career, marked by numerous achievements and awards, had a lasting impact on the development of electron microscopy and its applications.'

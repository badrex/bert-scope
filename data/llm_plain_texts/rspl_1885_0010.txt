tldr: The author, James Hector, describes his observations of the total solar eclipse of September 9, 1885, focusing on the scarlet prominences and the corona. He reports on the irregular outline of the corona and the large expansion reaching nearly two diameters from the sun's limb. The author also mentions the intense flashes of light observed at the beginning and end of totality, which he compares to electric lights.

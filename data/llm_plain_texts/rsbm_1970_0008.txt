tldr: 'Charles Lovatt Evans was a prominent physiologist and biochemist who made significant contributions to the field of physiology. Born in Birmingham, he overcame various challenges to pursue a scientific career. He worked at the University of London and University College London, where he conducted research and taught. Lovatt Evans was a lifelong associate of E. H. Starling and made important discoveries in enzyme action and the study of hibernating glands. He was also involved in defensive measures against chemical warfare during World War I. Throughout his life, he remained connected to and supportive of the universities and medical schools he had worked with.'

tldr: The article is a biographical memoir of Howard Latimer Penman, a physicist and acoustician who made significant contributions to the fields of soil physics and hydrology. Penman's career spanned various research areas, including acoustics, spectroscopy, and the effects of temperature on sound dispersion. He worked at institutions such as Armstrong College, the Shirley Institute, and Rothamsted Experimental Station, where he made important discoveries in soil fumigation and water loss from bare soil.

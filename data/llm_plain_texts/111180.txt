tldr: This article presents the results of meteorological observations in the English Lake District, focusing on the fall of rain at various heights up to 3166 feet above sea level. The author, John Fletcher Miller, shares data for the year 1851 and discusses the daily fall of rain during an excessively wet month. He also highlights the permanently registering system for recording the returns from various stations.

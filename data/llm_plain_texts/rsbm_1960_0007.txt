tldr: 'Alexander Gibb was a prominent civil engineer who followed in the footsteps of his family, who were also civil engineers. He worked on various projects, including the King Edward VII bridge at Kew and the naval dockyard at Rosyth. Gibb also served as Civil Engineer-in-Chief at the Admiralty, where he worked on schemes to counter the U-boat menace during World War I. His contributions to engineering and infrastructure development left a lasting impact on the field.'

tldr: This biographical memoir recounts the life of Alfred Charles Glyn Egerton, a prominent figure in the scientific community during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Born into a family with a rich scientific heritage, Egerton pursued his passion for science and made significant contributions to the field of chemistry. He studied at Eton College and University College, working under notable scientists such as Thomas Cunningham Porter and Sir William Ramsay. Throughout his life, Egerton maintained a balance between his scientific pursuits and his involvement in public service, serving as an instructor at the Royal Military Academy, Woolwich, and contributing to various scientific organizations, including the Royal Society.

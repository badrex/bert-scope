tldr: This article is an obituary for Francis Alexander Towle, who served as the Assistant Secretary of the Royal Society from 1921 until his death in 1932. Towle was a devoted and loyal employee who maintained the Society's traditions and oversaw its administrative tasks. He played a crucial role in helping the Society navigate a difficult period and was instrumental in improving the Society's facilities and care for its collection of fine portraits.

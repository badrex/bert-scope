tldr: This article describes a trigonometrical survey conducted in England between 1797 and 1799, ordered by Marquis Cornwallis. Captain William Mudge led the survey, which covered Essex, western Kent, Suffolk, Hertfordshire, and parts of neighboring counties. The survey included the measurement of a new baseline on Sedgemoor and the determination of latitudes, longitudes, and meridians for mapping purposes.

tldr: James Alfred Ewing was a prominent engineer and scientist who made significant contributions to magnetism, seismology, and earthquake research. He worked in Japan for five years, where he developed a new seismograph that recorded continuous earthquake motion. Ewing later returned to Scotland and became a professor of engineering at the University of Dundee, where he established the Engineering School and helped to develop engineering education in Cambridge. His work and influence continue to be remembered and celebrated today.

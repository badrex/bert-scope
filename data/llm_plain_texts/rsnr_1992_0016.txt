tldr: This article reviews a book about Richard Rawlinson, a Non-juring Jacobite who was elected as a Fellow of the Royal Society in 1714. The book provides a detailed account of Rawlinson's life, his family, and the history of the Rawlinson-Bosworth Chair. The review also discusses European imperialism in the 19th century, its nature, scope, and timing, and the various explanations for its development, including economic, strategic, political, military, and humanitarian interests.

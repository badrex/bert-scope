tldr: This article examines the early membership and financial records of the Royal Society, focusing on the account of William Balle from November 1660 to September 1663. It discusses the evolution of the Society's admission process, membership dues, and the gradual establishment of a routine for candidate admission. The article also highlights the financial transactions and obligations of the early members.

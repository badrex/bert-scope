tldr: The article is a biographical memoir of Leslie Hugh Norman Cooper, a prominent chemist and oceanographer. It highlights his early life, education, and career, focusing on his significant contributions to the field of marine biology and oceanography. Cooper is best known for his work on nutrient analysis in seawater, his research on phytoplankton and zooplankton populations, and his role in developing the modern concepts of factors controlling phytoplankton populations. His dedication and tenacity in overcoming challenges in his research made him a key figure in the Plymouth Laboratory of the Marine Biological Association.

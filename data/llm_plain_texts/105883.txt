tldr: This article presents observations of weather, wind, and frost made at Prince of Wales's Fort on the North-West Coast of Hudson's Bay in the years 1768 and 1769 by Joseph Dymond and William Wales. The authors recorded various weather conditions, including temperature, wind speed, and precipitation, as well as the effects of frost on structures and objects. These observations provide valuable historical data for understanding the climate and weather patterns of the region.

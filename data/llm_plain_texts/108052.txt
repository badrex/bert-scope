tldr: In 1834, James Clark Ross embarked on an expedition to determine the position of the North Magnetic Pole. His journey involved navigating through challenging Arctic terrain and making accurate magnetic observations. Although Ross did not reach the exact magnetic pole, his findings contributed significantly to the understanding of Earth's magnetic field and the advancement of polar exploration.

tldr: The article is a biographical memoir of Archibald Vivian Hill, a renowned British physiologist. Born in Bristol, Hill was a pioneer in the field of muscle physiology and biophysics. He studied at Blundell's School and Trinity College, Cambridge, where he specialized in mathematics and later shifted to physiology under the influence of Walter Fletcher. Hill made significant contributions to the understanding of muscle metabolism and was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1922. He played a crucial role in establishing the field of biophysics and was a prominent figure at the Cambridge Physiological Laboratory.

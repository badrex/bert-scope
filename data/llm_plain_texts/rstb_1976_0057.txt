tldr: This article reviews the United Kingdom's contribution to the International Biological Programme, focusing on nutrition and human adaptability studies. It discusses various investigations on diverse populations, including Tristan da Cunha, New Guinea, Israel, Malawi, Tanzania, and Sudan. The studies explored energy and protein requirements, environmental factors, and the adaptability of individuals to different living conditions. The findings have influenced our understanding of nutrition and its role in human adaptability.

tldr: Edmund Beecher Wilson was a pioneering American biologist and educator who made significant contributions to the fields of zoology, genetics, and evolution. He studied at Antioch College, Yale, and Johns Hopkins University before becoming a professor at various institutions, including Williams College, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, and Bryn Mawr College. Wilson is best known for his research on Renilla, his work in Naples at the zoological station, and his collaboration with Adam Sedgwick on a popular biology textbook.

tldr: This article discusses satellite observations of the Antarctic atmosphere, focusing on the stratosphere and mesosphere. It highlights the importance of studying the behavior of the Southern Hemisphere's atmosphere, particularly in winter, using data from instruments like the selective chopper radiometer and pressure modulator radiometer. The article also explores various methods for observing and measuring the atmosphere, including cloud pictures, wind at cloud heights, and the net radiation budget.

tldr: The article discusses the relationship between Antoni van Leeuwenhoek and the Royal Society, focusing on his significant contributions to the understanding of blood circulation. Leeuwenhoek's observation of blood circulation in frogs' larvae and his correspondence with the Royal Society led to the acceptance of William Harvey's theory of circulation. Despite his lack of formal scientific education, Leeuwenhoek's experiments and discoveries were highly regarded by fellow scientists and members of the Royal Society.

tldr: 'The article describes the silicification process in two types of protistan flagellates, Synura petersenii (Chrysophyceae) and Stephanoeca diplocostata (Choanoflagellida). Both species produce silica units intracellularly and assemble them outside the protoplast to form a cell wall. The study provides detailed comparisons of silica deposition between the two taxa and other silica-depositing protistans, contributing to the understanding of their unique biogenesis and ecological roles.'

tldr: The author, Everard Home, describes the unique circulation of blood in the Vermes class of Linnaeus and compares it with other animal classes. The central point of circulation in Vermes is different from other animals, and the muscular structure of branchial vessels plays a significant role in the circulation. The author also discusses the differences in blood circulation and aeration in Lumbricus terrestris and Sepia, highlighting the unique features of each.

tldr: This article investigates the relationship between human and canine Leishmaniasis, focusing on the causative organism, Leishmania infantum, and its effects on dogs in Mediterranean regions. The authors discuss the symptoms, transmission, and pathology of canine visceral Leishmaniasis, as well as the role of sandflies in the spread of the disease. They also describe the process of diagnosing the disease through spleen examination and the histological changes observed in infected dogs.

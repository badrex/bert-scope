tldr: The author describes the unique method of making ice in Benares, India, where ice is produced without boiling water or extremely low temperatures. The process involves filling shallow, porous earth pans with water, placing them on straw beds, and allowing the ice to form during the night. The author also discusses the relationship between temperature and ice formation, noting that ice can form even when the thermometer is above the freezing point.

tldr: 'Sir Martin Ryle was a pioneering radio astronomer who made significant contributions to the field, including the invention of aperture synthesis in radio telescopes and the advancement of observational cosmology. Born into a family with a history of scientific and academic achievements, Ryle's early interests were in radio and physics. He later became Astronomer Royal and a Nobel laureate, working on radar countermeasures during World War II before turning his focus to radio astronomy and its applications in understanding the cosmos.'

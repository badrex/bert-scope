tldr: This article describes an ancient shrine found at Croyland Abbey, believed to be of Saxon origin. The shrine, made of oak and plated with copper, features engravings and enamelled blue ground. The author, Wm. Stukely, discusses the history of the shrine and its possible connection to the murder of Abbot Theodore and his monks by the Vikings in 870 AD. The author also compares the shrine to other similar relics and their destruction during the Dissolution of Monasteries.

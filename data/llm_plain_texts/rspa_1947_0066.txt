tldr: The authors conducted experiments in a Wilson chamber to study sublimation and ice formation at low temperatures. They discovered that only liquid water droplets form at higher temperatures, but below a certain threshold temperature, ice particles begin to form through direct sublimation of water vapor. The experiments also showed that in dust-free air, there are no sublimation nuclei for ice formation at temperatures between 0 and -32Â°C. The presence of negative ions and X-rays increased the density of ice particles, suggesting that they act as genuine sublimation nuclei.

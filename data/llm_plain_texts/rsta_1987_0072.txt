tldr: This article discusses the advancements and applications of accelerator mass spectrometry (AMS) in radiocarbon dating. AMS allows for smaller sample sizes and greater selectivity, enabling more precise and reliable dating. The author highlights the challenges and technical aspects of AMS, emphasizing the importance of maintaining high reproducibility and accuracy in measurements. The potential of AMS in various fields, such as archaeology, geology, and environmental science, is also explored.

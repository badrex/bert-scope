tldr: This article discusses the advantages and limitations of high-voltage electron microscopy in biology, focusing on the increased specimen penetration and improved image resolution. It also explores the challenges of contrast decrease and response of viewing screens and photographic emulsions at higher voltages. The article outlines the potential applications of high-voltage microscopy in examining living organisms and thick specimens, as well as the differences in construction and operation between high-voltage and standard electron microscopes.

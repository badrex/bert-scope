tldr: The article examines the fauna found in the Hoxnian interglacial deposits of Nechells, Birmingham. The fauna consists of a small number of species, including fish, ostracods, Cladocera, mites, molluscs, and polyzoa, with beetles being the most predominant. The study provides insights into the climate and environment during the Hoxnian interglacial period and helps to understand the distribution of species and their adaptations to changing conditions.

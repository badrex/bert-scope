tldr: This article is a biographical memoir of Frank Leonard Engledow, a prominent figure in the fields of agriculture and biology. Born in 1890, Engledow made significant contributions to plant breeding, biometry, and genetics. He studied at the University of London and Cambridge, where he developed an interest in the application of mathematics to biology. Engledow worked at the School of Agriculture and Plant Breeding Institute, where he used his mathematical and statistical skills to advance agricultural research. He served in World War I and later became a professor at Cambridge University. His work had a lasting impact on the fields of agriculture and biology.

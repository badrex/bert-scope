Born in Swansea, Wales, he was influenced by his family's background in education and went on to study geology at the University College of Swansea and St John's College, Cambridge. George's research focused on the Carboniferous period, and he made significant contributions to the fields of geology and palaeontology. His dedication to education and the study of Gower's rocks also played a significant role in his life and career.'

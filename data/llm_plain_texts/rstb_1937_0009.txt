tldr: The Acanthodian fishes are a unique group of vertebrate fossils, characterized by their square, minute ganoid scales and large anterior spines supporting their fins. They were diverse and widespread during the Lower Devonian period but declined in variety by the Carboniferous times. This article provides a detailed study of their structure, classification, and evolution.

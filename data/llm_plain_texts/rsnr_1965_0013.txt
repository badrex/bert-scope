tldr: This article discusses John Locke's review of Newton's Principia, published in Jean Leclerc's Bibliotheque Universelle. Locke, a qualified reviewer due to his involvement in the experimental philosophy, appreciated Newton's use of the geometrical method in mechanics and praised the Principia as a groundbreaking work. The review also highlights Locke's dissatisfaction with Descartes's philosophy and his anticipation for a new system of natural philosophy.

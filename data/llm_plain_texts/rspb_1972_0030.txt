tldr: This article discusses the challenges in determining water quality criteria for freshwater fisheries and the importance of protecting them from the effects of industrial and domestic effluents. It evaluates the use and limitations of laboratory tests, sublethal effects, and field observations in preparing water quality criteria. The author emphasizes the need for a comprehensive approach considering various factors, such as chemical and physical variables, different fish species, and the effects of pollutant mixtures.

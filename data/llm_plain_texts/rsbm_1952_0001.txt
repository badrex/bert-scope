He studied at Cambridge and worked with notable figures like Graham Kerr and S. F. Harmer. Agar conducted research on the development of the skull, chromosome behavior, and the inheritance of traits in water fleas. His discoveries contributed to the understanding of genetics and the mechanisms of heredity.'

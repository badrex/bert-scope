tldr: The author examines the unique structure of Chitons, a type of mollusk, and their series of imbricated valves. They discover that Chitons are normal Gastropods with a series of more or less rudimentary valves in front of the usual shell. The author also discusses the additional protection provided by calcareous scales and spines, and the shell's structure and growth process.

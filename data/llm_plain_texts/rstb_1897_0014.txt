tldr: This article investigates the variability and mutual relationships of certain parts of the human skeleton, specifically focusing on the Naqada race discovered by Flinders Petrie in Egypt. The author conducted a series of measurements on the skeletons using an osteometric board and analyzed the limb bones, sacrum, and scapula. The study provides insights into the variability and similarities within the race, contributing to the understanding of human evolution and physical anthropology.

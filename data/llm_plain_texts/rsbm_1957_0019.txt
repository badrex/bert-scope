tldr: This article is a biographical memoir of John William Trevan, a prominent figure in medical research and pharmacology. Born in Cornwall, Trevan had a successful career as a medical student at St Bartholomew's Hospital before transitioning to laboratory work. He held various positions in the field, including pharmacologist at the Welcome Physiological Research Laboratories, and contributed significantly to the development of sulphonamides and other drugs. Trevan was also involved in various organizations and held several prestigious titles throughout his career. He passed away unexpectedly in 1956.

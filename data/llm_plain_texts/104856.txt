tldr: The author examines the formation and characteristics of pebbles in Norfolk County, observing their broken pieces, sharp edges, and worn surfaces. He also discusses the presence of various types of marble, sandstone, and gypsum within these pebbles. The author notes the differences in hardness and brittleness between pebbles found near the Earth's surface and those found deeper within the Earth. He also highlights the role of sands and pebbles in draining moisture and the use of clay and ferrugineous crusts to maintain the separation between different strata.

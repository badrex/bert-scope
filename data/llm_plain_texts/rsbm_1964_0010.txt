tldr: Edward David Hughes was a prominent Welsh chemist known for his work in organic chemistry, particularly the mechanisms of prototropy and reactions of saturated aliphatic compounds. He collaborated with C.K. Ingold for 33 years and held various positions at University College London and Bangor. Hughes also contributed to isotope separation and organic compounds of higher group-IV elements during his career.

tldr: The article is an obituary for Heike Kamerlingh Onnes, a renowned Dutch physicist who made significant contributions to low-temperature research. He developed innovative cooling systems and techniques, liquefied helium for the first time, and discovered the phenomenon of superconductivity in metals at extremely low temperatures. Onnes founded the famous cryogenic laboratory at Leiden University and trained many skilled investigators in his field.

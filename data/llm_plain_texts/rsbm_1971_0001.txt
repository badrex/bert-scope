tldr: The article is a biographical memoir of Abram Samoilovitch Besicovitch, a prominent mathematician known for his work in geometry, measure theory, and analysis. Born into a Karaim family, Besicovitch showed an aptitude for mathematics from a young age. He studied at the University of St. Petersburg and later became a professor at various institutions in Russia and the UK. His research focused on topics such as periodic functions, geometric properties of plane sets, and Hausdorff measure. Besicovitch was a dedicated teacher and mentor, guiding the early research of several notable mathematicians. He was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society and received numerous awards for his contributions to mathematics.

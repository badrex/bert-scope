tldr: The article highlights the accomplishments of several scientists who were awarded Royal Society Medals in 1962. Sir Cyril Hinshelwood is recognized for his work on chemical kinetics and bacterial growth. Professor D. M. Newitt is celebrated for his research in chemical engineering, particularly at high pressures. Professor S. Chandrasekhar is commended for his mathematical investigations into fluid dynamics and hydrodynamics. Sir John Eccles is praised for his studies on the mammalian spinal cord and synaptic transmission. Professor H. J. Emeleus is honored for his contributions to inorganic chemistry, including the discovery of new compounds and the revival of interest in the field in Great Britain.

tldr: The author describes the fossil remains of a gigantic land-lizard, Megalania prisca, found in Australia. The three vertebrae analyzed belonged to a large reptile of the Lacertian order, closely related to existing Australian monitor lizards. The author discusses the differences and similarities between the extinct Megalania and existing monitor lizards, as well as the significance of the procoelian type of vertebra in the reptile class.

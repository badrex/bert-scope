tldr: This article explores the relationship between the English monarchy and the rise of science in 17th century England. It highlights the challenges faced by scientists during this period, including a lack of support and financial resources from the government. The author argues that the 1690s marked a formative stage in the new science's rise to widespread acceptance, despite the disruptions caused by political events. The article also discusses the role of the Royal Society and its members in promoting and advancing scientific knowledge during this time.

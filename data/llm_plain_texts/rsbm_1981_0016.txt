tldr: This article is a biographical memoir of Ronald George Wreyford Norrish, a prominent chemist known for his work in photochemistry, chain reactions, and polymer chemistry. Born in Cambridge, Norrish spent his entire life in academia, serving as a professor of physical chemistry at the University of Cambridge. He was a pioneer in photochemistry and made significant contributions to the field, including the development of flash photolysis as a means of studying rapid chemical reactions. For this work, he shared the Nobel Prize for Chemistry in 1967. The article covers Norrish's life, education, and scientific achievements, highlighting his dedication to research and his impact on the field of chemistry.

tldr: This article discusses the background and refereeing of R. A. Fisher's 1918 paper on the correlation between relatives on the supposition of Mendelian inheritance. It provides insights into the historical context of the controversy between biometricians and Mendelian theorists and presents the referees' reports on Fisher's paper. The article highlights the importance of Fisher's work in synthesizing biometric results with Mendelian theory and its impact on the field of genetics.

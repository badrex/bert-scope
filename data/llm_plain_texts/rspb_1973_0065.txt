tldr: The article discusses the advancements and potential future of automation in healthcare, focusing on the clinical chemistry laboratory, medical diagnostics, and the need for more accessible and affordable technology. It highlights the increasing demand for rapid serial estimations to assess the results of deliberate perturbations of body function and the need for ward or outpatient-based analyzers working in real-time. The article also touches upon the challenges of automation's effects on other laboratory fields, such as haematology, microbiology, and histopathology, and the potential applications of automation and computers in radiology, radiotherapy, and radioisotope applications.

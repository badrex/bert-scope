tldr: Roderick Oliver Redman was a prominent British astronomer and astrophysicist, born in 1905. He attended Marling School and later St John's College, Cambridge, where he studied mathematics and physics. Redman worked under the guidance of renowned astronomer Arthur Eddington and conducted research on binary stars, galactic rotation, and spectroscopic orbits. He held various positions, including Assistant Astronomer at the Dominion Astrophysical Observatory and Assistant Director of the Solar Physics Observatory at Cambridge. Redman's contributions to the field of astronomy and his life story are celebrated in this biographical memoir.

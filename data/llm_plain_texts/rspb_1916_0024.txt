tldr: The author investigates the effects of injecting saline solutions and other fluids to restore arterial pressure after blood loss. They found that saline solutions are relatively ineffective due to their low viscosity, which leads to a decrease in arterial pressure. The author suggests using alternative fluids with higher viscosity and lower osmotic pressure, such as gum acacia or gelatin, to improve the restoration of arterial pressure.

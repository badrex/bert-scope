tldr: This article discusses the historical debate over the intellectual origins of the Royal Society, focusing on the contributions of London and Oxford groups. The author argues that London was the center where scientific ideas mainly originated in 16th and 17th-century England, and the Royal Society should be called the Royal Society of London. The article also highlights the significance of Francis Bacon's influence on the development of the new system of natural philosophy.

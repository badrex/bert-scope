tldr: The President of the Royal Society, Sir Alan Hodgkin, delivers the Anniversary Address, highlighting the achievements and contributions of various scientists who received medals in 1974. The recipients include Sir William Hodge for his work in algebraic geometry, Sir Alan Cottrell for his contributions to structural metallurgy, Fred Hoyle for his work in astronomy, Dr. Sydney Brenner for his contributions to molecular biology, Sir George Edwards for his work in aviation, Professor J. Baddiley for his research in organic chemistry, Professor P. M. Sheppard for his work on genetic consequences of natural selection, and Professor P. H. Fowler for his contributions to particle physics and cosmic ray research.

tldr: The Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew, established by Princess Augusta, has a rich history of plant research and collection. Serving as a pioneer in botanical gardens, Kew has grown to become an international center for plant research, housing over five million herbarium specimens and providing valuable services to science. The gardens have expanded over time, with the help of notable figures like Sir Joseph Banks and Sir Joseph Hooker, and now serve as a crucial resource for plant taxonomy and botanical research.

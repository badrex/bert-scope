tldr: This article discusses the life and work of Richard Owen, a prominent figure in Victorian natural history and comparative anatomy. Owen was a skilled anatomist, public lecturer, paleontologist, and taxonomist who played a significant role in the establishment of the Natural History Museum in South Kensington. The article explores Owen's various achievements, including his work on cataloging John Hunter's collection, his research on rare animals, and his involvement in London's scientific society. It also highlights his controversies with other prominent scientists like Charles Darwin and Thomas Henry Huxley.

tldr: This article discusses the discoveries and observations of cosmic gamma-ray bursts, focusing on their time structures, spectra, and distribution. The authors describe the advancements in locating these bursts with greater accuracy using a network of observations from various satellites. They also explore the possible origins and emission mechanisms of gamma-ray bursts, as well as the constraints these new findings place on existing models.

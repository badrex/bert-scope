tldr: This article discusses various modeling techniques used to simulate and predict the movement of nitrate from agricultural sources to public water supplies. The authors focus on the Thames River basin and present a semi-distributed approach that includes a hydrological model, a soil zone and aquifer model, and an integrated model of flow and water quality. The study highlights the impact of agricultural practices on nitrate levels in rivers and the importance of understanding these processes to address environmental pollution and maintain soil productivity.

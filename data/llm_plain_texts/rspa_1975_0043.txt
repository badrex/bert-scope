tldr: This article discusses the effects of World War II on the development of knowledge in the biological sciences. It highlights how the war was beneficial to biology, as it allowed biologists to contribute to the war effort and apply their knowledge to various problems. The war also led to the development of penicillin as a chemotherapeutic agent and the study of blood groups. The article emphasizes the lasting impact of wartime experiences on biologists and the significant role they played in post-war biological research.

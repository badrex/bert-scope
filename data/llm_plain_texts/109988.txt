tldr: This article discusses the differences between the Manatee of the West Indies and the Dugong of the East Indian Seas. Sir Everard Home highlights differences in their external features, such as tail shape, presence of tusks and nails, and snout form. He also notes differences in their teeth, number of vertebrae, and stomach structure. These findings emphasize the importance of considering more than just teeth when classifying animals.

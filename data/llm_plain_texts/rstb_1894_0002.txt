tldr: The author describes the discovery of a unique skull belonging to the extinct mammal Megaladapis madagascariensis, found in Madagascar. This gigantic lemuroid species had a distinct skull structure with a broad interorbital region, large orbits, and a short, low braincase. The author discusses the differences between this skull and the typical Lemurid skull, suggesting a closer relationship with Marsupials and Insectivores.

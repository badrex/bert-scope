tldr: The author conducted observations of the diurnal variation of the magnetic needle at Fort Marlborough, Sumatra, and found that the east variation increased from morning to afternoon and decreased until morning. The author discusses the possible causes of this variation, such as heat affecting the magnetic poles, and suggests further observations to better understand this phenomenon.

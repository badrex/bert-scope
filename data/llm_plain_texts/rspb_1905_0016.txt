tldr: The author examines the brains of humans and animals infected with various forms of trypanosomes, focusing on Sleeping Sickness. They observe chronic meningo-encephalitis and inflammation in the brains, along with the presence of trypanosomes or their remnants. The author discusses the possible reasons for the chronic inflammation and the role of secondary infections in the disease process. The study provides insights into the pathogenesis of trypanosome infections and their effects on the central nervous system.

tldr: This article highlights the contributions of Charles Drummond Ellis to nuclear physics, focusing on his work in nuclear spectroscopy and the discovery of the neutrino. Ellis, often overlooked in the history of physics, was a crucial part of Rutherford's team at the Cavendish Laboratory and made significant contributions to the field. His meticulous approach to experimental physics and dedication to obtaining reliable results made him an essential collaborator in the development of nuclear physics.

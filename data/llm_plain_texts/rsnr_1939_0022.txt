tldr: This article describes the unique and beautiful display of wildflowers in the coastal strip of the Libyan desert, specifically in the Mariut region. The author explains the factors that contribute to this phenomenon, such as the care-free agricultural methods of the Bedouins, the amount and spacing of winter rains, and the topography of the area. The author also discusses the importance of rainfall and soil composition for the growth of both wild and cultivated plants in the region.

tldr: The article provides a historical introduction to the Large Electron-Positron Collider (LEP) at CERN, discussing its development, design, and construction. LEP was a groundbreaking facility for high-energy physics, enabling the detection of Z-events and paving the way for further research in particle physics. The article highlights the challenges faced during the project, including technical and financial issues, as well as the collaboration and dedication of physicists, engineers, and technicians involved in its realization.

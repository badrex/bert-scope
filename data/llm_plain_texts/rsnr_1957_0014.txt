tldr: The Royal Society delegation visited the Soviet Union in 1956 to establish better contacts with Soviet scientists and explore their research institutes. They visited Moscow, Leningrad, and Kiev, attending banquets, sightseeing, and visiting research institutes and museums. The delegation found a vast effort in teaching and training scientists, with a focus on research in physics, mathematics, chemistry, and biology. The visit aimed to foster international collaboration and cultural exchange in the scientific community.

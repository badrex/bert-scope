tldr: The author examines the organization and growth of fossil plants from the coal measures, specifically focusing on the Lycopodiaceae family. They describe the structure and development of the vascular system and bark in these plants, as well as the presence of exogenous growth layers. The author also discusses the strobili, sporangia, and macrospores found in these plants, and compares their structure to that of living Lycopodiaceae.

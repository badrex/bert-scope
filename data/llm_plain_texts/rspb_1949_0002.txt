tldr: In his annual address, Sir Robert Robinson highlights the achievements of four distinguished scientists who received awards from the Royal Society. Archibald Vivian Hill is awarded the Copley Medal for his contributions to the physiology of muscular processes, Franz Eugen Simon receives the Royal Medal for his research on low-temperature properties of matter, Harold Jeffreys is awarded the Royal Medal for his investigations in theoretical geomechanics, and James Gray receives the Royal Medal for his work on the mechanism of posture and locomotion in vertebrates. Edmund Langley Hirst is awarded the Davy Medal for his contributions to carbohydrate chemistry.

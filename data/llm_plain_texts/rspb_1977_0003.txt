tldr: This article discusses recent advancements in medical endoscopy, focusing on new clinical procedures in the endoscopy of the digestive tract. It highlights the development of long forward-viewing endoscopes, biopsy and cytology facilities, and the ability to perform inversion maneuvers within the stomach. The article also covers the importance of tissue diagnosis, mucosal inspection, and new procedures like bouginage, passage through strictures, and variceal injection. Additionally, it explores the use of endoscopic polypectomy, foreign-body removal, and dye injection in the stomach. The article emphasizes the significance of combined oesophago-gastro-duodenoscopy and the examination of the pylorus and duodenal bulb in understanding and treating ulcer diseases.

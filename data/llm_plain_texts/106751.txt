tldr: In this article, M. Francis de Zach describes his observations of the lunar eclipse and vernal equinox in 1783. He discusses the equipment and methods used, such as the achromatic telescope and the importance of accurate timekeeping. He also touches upon the calculation of orbits of comets and the significance of the moon's spots in determining longitude. The observations were made in collaboration with Father Le Fevre and M. Saint Jacques de Sylvabelle.

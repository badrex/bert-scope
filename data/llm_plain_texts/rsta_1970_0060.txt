tldr: The article discusses the development of photoelectron spectroscopy, specifically electron spectroscopy for chemical analysis (ESCA), which allows for the direct observation of electrons in matter. The technique is useful for studying the electronic structure of atoms, molecules, and solid materials, and has applications in various fields, including chemistry, biology, and materials science. The article highlights the advantages and limitations of ESCA and provides examples of its use in analyzing molecular and solid state structures, as well as surface chemistry.

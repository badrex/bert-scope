tldr: 'This biographical memoir recounts the life and career of Paul Taunton Matthews, a prominent figure in post-war British theoretical physics. Born in India to missionary parents, Matthews made significant contributions to the field of quantum field theory and quantum mechanics. He played a crucial role in the revival of British theoretical physics and held various positions in academia, including head of the Physics Department at Imperial College and vice-chancellor of the University of Bath. Matthews was also an advocate for peace and nuclear disarmament.'

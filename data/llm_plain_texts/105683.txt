tldr: In 1761, Dr. W. Watson observed and described the catarrhal disorder and dysentery prevalent in London and its surroundings. He discussed the symptoms, affected population, and recommended treatments for these diseases. Watson also shared his experience with other physicians' works and observations on similar diseases. He believed that the cold and frosty weather might have stopped the spread of dysentery.

tldr: This biographical memoir highlights the life and work of John Hasbrouck Van Vleck, a renowned physicist who made significant contributions to the field of magnetism. His work in applying quantum mechanics to the theory of electric and magnetic susceptibilities, as well as establishing the detailed theory of paramagnetism on a firm quantum mechanical basis, has had a lasting impact on the study of magnetism. Van Vleck's career included positions at Harvard University and the University of Wisconsin, and he was awarded the Nobel Prize for Physics in 1977.

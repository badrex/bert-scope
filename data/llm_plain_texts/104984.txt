tldr: Dr. Vitaliano Donati, a Professor of Botany, shares his observations and experiences of earthquakes in Turin in 1755 and 1756. He discusses the possible connection between earthquakes and atmospheric phenomena, such as changes in the air and electrical forces. Donati also mentions the role of temperature and barometric pressure during these events and shares his experiments with a brass wire and a leaden bullet to study the motion of earthquakes.

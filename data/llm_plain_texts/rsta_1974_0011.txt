tldr: The article discusses the advanced astronomy and timekeeping practices of the ancient Maya civilization. The Maya had three concurrent counts for their calendar system, including a 260-day sacred almanac and a 365-day year. They made significant astronomical observations, such as predicting eclipses and tracking the movements of Venus and the Moon. The Maya's complex calendar system and astronomical knowledge were crucial to their culture and daily life.

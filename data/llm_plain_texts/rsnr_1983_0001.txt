tldr: This article explores Joseph Priestley's life and scientific contributions during his visits to London. Priestley, a prominent figure in the fields of science, religion, and politics, spent time in London attending meetings at the Royal Society, conducting experiments, and meeting influential figures like Benjamin Franklin and Richard Price. His work on electricity and chemistry, including the discovery of fixed air (carbon dioxide) and inflammable air (hydrogen), earned him a place in the Royal Society and contributed to the development of scientific knowledge during the 18th century.

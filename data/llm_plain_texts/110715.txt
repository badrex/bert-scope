tldr: The author, T. Wharton Jones, F.R.S., clarifies the historical context of the discovery of the germinal vesicle in mammiferous ova. He explains that he was an independent discoverer of the vesicle, but was not the first. He points out that his paper was presented to the Royal Society in 1835, contemporaneous with Professor Rudolph Wagner's publication. Jones emphasizes the importance of acknowledging the correct historical precedence in scientific discoveries.

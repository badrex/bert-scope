Trained as a chemist, Hill applied his skills to understand living things, establishing biochemistry as an independent discipline. He was also an artist and craftsman, growing plants for his experiments and creating his own pigments for his watercolor sketches. Hill spent his entire career in Cambridge, where he made important discoveries and influenced future generations of scientists.'

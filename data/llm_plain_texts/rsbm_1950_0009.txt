tldr: Charles Stanley Gibson was a prominent chemist and educator who made significant contributions to the field of organic chemistry. He received his education at Manchester Grammar School and Corpus Christi College, Oxford, before joining Professor W.J. Pope at the Municipal College of Technology in Manchester. Gibson later moved to Cambridge to work with Pope and made important discoveries in asymmetric phosphonium compounds. He also played a vital role in reorganizing the Maharajah's College at Trivandrum, India, and contributed to the war effort during World War I. Gibson was a dedicated member of the Chemical Society and served in various capacities, including as an Honorary Secretary and Vice-President. He was also involved in the Academic Assistance Council and the British Association, and worked as a gas adviser during World War II.

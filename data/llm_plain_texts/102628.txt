tldr: The author provides an account of Dr. Wallis's third volume of his Opera Mathematica, which focuses on preserving and restoring ancient Greek authors and mathematical works. The volume includes translations and commentaries on Ptolemy's Harmonics, Archimedes' Arenarius, and other significant mathematical texts. The author also discusses the methods used by Wallis for teaching deaf and dumb individuals to speak and understand language, as well as his work on correcting speech impediments.

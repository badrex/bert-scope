tldr: Archibald Byron Macallum was a prominent Canadian biologist and educator who significantly contributed to the transformation of Toronto's Medical School into one of the top institutions in North America. He conducted groundbreaking research in microchemical investigations of inorganic salts in cells and the quantitative determination of ions in body fluids. Macallum's influence extended to the establishment of the National Research Council and his work in biochemistry and physiology left a lasting impact on the scientific community.

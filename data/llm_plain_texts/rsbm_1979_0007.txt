tldr: 'James Craigie was a prominent microbiologist who contributed significantly to the development of virology and bacteriology in Canada. His research focused on vaccinia, variola, and typhoid, leading to advancements in understanding viral antigens, antibodies, and bacterial flagella. Craigie's work on bacteriophages and their application in typing typhoid strains proved to be of great epidemiological value. He also contributed to the study of poliomyelitis and typhus during World War II, working on vaccines and other research projects.'

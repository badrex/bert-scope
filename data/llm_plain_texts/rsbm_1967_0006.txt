tldr: This biographical memoir recounts the life and career of Dalziel Llewellyn Hammick, a prominent chemistry educator and researcher. Born in London in 1887, Hammick excelled in both academics and sports during his youth. He studied chemistry at Oxford, where he developed a passion for teaching and research. After working in Munich and serving in World War I, Hammick held various teaching and tutoring positions at prestigious schools and colleges, including Gresham's School, Winchester College, Oriel College, and Corpus Christi College. He was known for his ability to inspire students and his contributions to the fields of organic and physical chemistry. Hammick's life was marked by his love for teaching, humor, and appreciation of history and the humanities.

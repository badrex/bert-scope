tldr: This article presents a detailed study of the bacterial flora of the Thames River, focusing on the growth, behavior, and taxonomy of 80 different bacterial forms. The author discusses the challenges of identifying and classifying species based on macroscopic methods and proposes a new approach to grouping bacterial forms into types based on their characteristics in various media. The study provides valuable insights into the complexity of bacterial life in aquatic environments and the importance of understanding their variations and relationships.

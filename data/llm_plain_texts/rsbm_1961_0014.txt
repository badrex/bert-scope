tldr: 'Charles Edward Kenneth Mees was a pioneering figure in the field of photographic science and technology. His work focused on the development of the photographic process, the relationship between the photograph and the subject, and the application of photography to scientific and technical investigations. Mees, along with Samuel Edward Sheppard, expanded on the work of Ferdinand Hurter and V. G. Driffield, and made significant contributions to the understanding of the sensitivity of photographic materials and the development of the photographic image.'

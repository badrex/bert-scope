tldr: This article discusses the lives and work of Richard Jack, a mathematical teacher, and Henry Baker, a microscopist, in the late 17th and early 18th centuries. It focuses on a letter from Alexander Hume-Campbell to Henry Baker, which reveals their shared interest in astronomy and sunspots. The letter also mentions Jack's involvement in a Court of Enquiry about the conduct of Lieutenant General Sir John Cope during the Jacobite Rebellion in 1745.

tldr: This article discusses the advancements in microcrystalline catalysts, which are microporous and have active sites distributed uniformly throughout their bulk. These catalysts can catalyze the chemical conversions of various gaseous molecules, especially hydrocarbons. The deployment of experimental and computational tools has provided insights into the mechanisms of catalytic action and suggested new strategies for designing tailored selective catalysts.

tldr: This article explores the Royal Society's interest in ginseng from 1666 to 1788, examining the various periods when attention was focused on the plant. It discusses how ginseng specimens reached Europe via the East India Company and how the Royal Society's interest was sparked by accounts from Jesuit missionaries and other European authors. The article also highlights the experiments and trials conducted by members of the Royal Society, such as Dr. Andrew Clench and Dr. Robert Witty, who used ginseng in medical practice.

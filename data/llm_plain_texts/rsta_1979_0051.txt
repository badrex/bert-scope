tldr: This article discusses the limitations and future prospects of ultrasonic and radiological methods in engineering and medical diagnosis. It explores the use of techniques such as close-range X-ray photogrammetry, cold neutron photogrammetry, and fast microdensitometer systems in analyzing engine movements and identifying issues in gas turbines. The authors also highlight the potential of neutron radiography in the gas turbine industry and its promising future applications.

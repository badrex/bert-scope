Born in 1851, Lodge studied at University College London and later became a professor of physics and mathematics. He is known for his work on electromagnetism, wireless telegraphy, and his influence on the development of higher education in the UK. Lodge was also a prolific writer, publishing numerous books and articles on science and its history.'

tldr: This article is a biographical memoir of James Norman Davidson, a prominent Scottish biochemist and medical researcher. Born in Edinburgh, Davidson studied at the University of Edinburgh and made significant contributions to the field of biochemistry, particularly in enzyme purification. He worked under the guidance of renowned scientists such as George Barger and Otto Warburg. Davidson's career highlights include his work on uricase and his appointment as a lecturer in biochemistry at the University of St Andrews.

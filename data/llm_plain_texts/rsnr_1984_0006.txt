tldr: This article discusses the establishment of the Industrial Fatigue Research Board during World War I, led by Sir Charles Sherrington, a renowned physiologist. Sherrington's experience in factory conditions and his practical approach to research made him an ideal candidate for the board. The article highlights his work on industrial fatigue, including his own experience working as an unskilled laborer in a shell factory, and the importance of understanding the natural laws of industrial fatigue for improving work conditions and overall health of workers.

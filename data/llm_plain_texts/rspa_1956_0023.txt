tldr: The President's address at the Royal Society's Anniversary Meeting in 1955 highlights the significant contributions of Ronald Fisher in quantitative biology and the advancements in the fields of genetics, evolution, and biometry. Additionally, the address recognizes the pioneering research in insect physiology by Vincent Brian Wigglesworth, and the outstanding achievements in organic chemistry, biochemistry, and nucleic acid chemistry by Alexander Todd. The address also mentions the awards given to H. W. Melville, E. C. Titchmarsh, H. S. W. Massey, and Sir Edward Salisbury for their remarkable work in various scientific disciplines.

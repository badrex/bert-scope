tldr: This article reviews the various techniques in transmission electron microscopy (TEM) used to study semiconductor interfaces. It highlights the strengths and characteristics of different methods, including high-resolution electron microscopy, lattice imaging, and convergent beam electron diffraction. The article also discusses recent advancements in TEM techniques, such as large-angle convergent beam diffraction and non-conventional imaging conditions, which have improved the understanding of semiconductor interfaces and their properties.

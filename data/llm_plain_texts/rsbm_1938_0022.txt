tldr: Thomas Mather was a pioneering engineer and educator in electrical engineering who contributed to the development of electrical standards, instruments, and laboratory training. He worked closely with Professor W.E. Ayrton and made significant improvements in the construction of electrical instruments, such as the Ayrton-Mather shunt and the electrostatic voltmeter. Mather's dedication to teaching and research in the field of electrical engineering left a lasting impact on the industry and the lives of many students and colleagues.

tldr: The article is an obituary for Richard Owen, a renowned comparative anatomist and paleontologist. Owen made significant contributions to the fields of anatomy, physiology, and paleontology, including the development of the concept of the vertebrate archetype, the distinction between homology and analogy, and the creation of an anatomical terminology system. He worked at the Royal College of Surgeons and the British Museum, conducting research on various animals and fossils, and authoring numerous publications.

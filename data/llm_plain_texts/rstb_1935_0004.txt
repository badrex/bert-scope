tldr: This study describes well-preserved vascular plants from Silurian rocks in Victoria, Australia, found alongside Monograptus graptolites. These plants, including Baragwanathia longifolia and Tarravia, are the oldest vascular plants known from any part of the world. The plants' preservation allows for accurate information about their form and structure to be gathered, providing valuable insights into ancient ecosystems.

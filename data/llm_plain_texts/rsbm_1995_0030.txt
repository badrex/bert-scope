tldr: 'Janet Watson was a prominent geologist and educator who made significant contributions to the understanding of Scottish Highland geology. She worked closely with John Sutton, and together they conducted groundbreaking research on the Lewisian, Torridonian, Moine, and Dalradian successions. Watson's expertise in sedimentary structures and structural geology helped shape modern interpretations of these formations. She also authored textbooks and supported the development of future geologists through her teaching and mentorship.'

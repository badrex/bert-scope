tldr: The Kew Observatory conducted various magnetic and meteorological observations in 1890, including magnetometer readings, magnetic diurnal variations, and sunspot observations. The results were deduced from magnetograph curves and standardized by the Astronomer Royal. These observations were crucial for understanding Earth's magnetic field and weather patterns.

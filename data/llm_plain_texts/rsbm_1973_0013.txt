tldr: 'Paul Gordon Fildes was a prominent microbiologist and bacteriologist who founded the study of bacterial nutrition. Born in London in 1882, Fildes made significant contributions to the field of medicine and health sciences. He worked with distinguished teachers and researchers, such as Adam Sedgwick, Walter Morley Fletcher, and William Bulloch, throughout his career. Fildes is best known for his research on bacterial nutrition and his work in the Department of Bacteriology at the London Hospital Medical College. His legacy continues to inspire and influence the field of microbiology and bacteriology today.'

tldr: This article discusses two portraits of James Short, a famous telescope-maker, and their connection to the history of art and science in Scotland. The author examines the paintings, one in the Royal Observatory, Edinburgh, and the other in the Museum of the History of Science, Oxford, and explores their significance in understanding Short's life and work. The article also delves into the role of David Erskine, Earl of Buchan, in collecting and documenting portraits of notable Scotsmen, including James Short.

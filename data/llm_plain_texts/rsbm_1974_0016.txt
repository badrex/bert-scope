tldr: 'Trevor Ian Shaw was a brilliant and dedicated scientist who made significant contributions to the fields of physiology, biophysics, and marine biology. He conducted groundbreaking research on sodium and potassium movements in red blood cells and the persistence of physiological rhythms in continuous daylight. Shaw was an inspiring teacher and a valuable colleague, known for his generosity, enthusiasm, and ability to generate original ideas and suggestions for new experiments. His untimely death in 1972 was a great loss to the scientific community.'

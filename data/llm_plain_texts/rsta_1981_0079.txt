tldr: This article discusses the generation and confinement of plasmas in tokamak systems, focusing on the advancements in plasma physics that have led to the potential development of fusion reactors. The author explains the basic tokamak configuration, the theory of plasma confinement, and the use of neutral atom beams for auxiliary heating. The article also highlights experimental results and the progress made in achieving high-temperature plasmas, which are crucial for the success of tokamak-based fusion reactors.

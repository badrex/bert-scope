tldr: The Royal Greenwich Observatory, established in 1675, has played a significant role in the field of meridian astronomy. The article discusses the history of the observatory, its contributions to the determination of positions and motions of celestial bodies, and the challenges faced in maintaining accurate observations. The observatory's work is essential for establishing a fundamental system of reference for the positions and proper motions of stars, which helps in understanding the Earth's motion and the structure of the universe.

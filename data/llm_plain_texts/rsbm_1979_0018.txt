tldr: 'The article is a biographical memoir of Thiruvenkata Rajendra Seshadri, a renowned Indian chemist, who made significant contributions to the fields of organic chemistry and natural products. Born in 1900 in South India, Seshadri received his education in local schools and colleges before studying chemistry at the prestigious Presidency College in Madras. He worked at various institutions, including the University of Manchester and University College London, where he conducted research on antimalarials and anthocyanins. Seshadri's dedication to his work, thirst for knowledge, and commitment to spirituality made him an influential figure in both science and education. He later became a professor and head of the Chemistry Department at Andhra University, where he trained many future researchers and helped establish the Department of Pharmaceutical Sciences.'

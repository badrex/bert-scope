tldr: This study describes the postcranial skeletons of the Triassic mammals Eozostrodon, Megazostrodon, and Erythrotherium. The research evaluates these mammals' skeletons in comparative and functional terms, revealing specializations in the atlas-axis complex that allowed extensive cranial flexion and rotation. The study also discusses the structure of cervical vertebrae, the shoulder and pelvic girdles, and the limb skeleton, providing insights into the habits and adaptations of these ancient mammals.

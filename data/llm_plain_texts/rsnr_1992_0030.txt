tldr: This article discusses the history of experimental philosophy and demonstrations at the Royal Society from its foundation in 1660 to the death of Isaac Newton in 1727. It covers the three main periods of the Society's history, highlighting the early optimistic Baconian phase, the middle period of the last quarter of the 17th century, and the years of Newton's Presidency. The article also explores the role of key figures like Robert Hooke and the decline of experimental demonstrations at the Society's meetings.

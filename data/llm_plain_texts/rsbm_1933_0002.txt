tldr: John Millar Thomson was a skilled chemist and educator who made significant contributions to chemistry education and research. He worked at King's College, London, and held various positions, including Vice-Principal and head of the Chemical Department. Thomson was also an active member of the Royal Society of Arts and the Chemical Society, where he served in various roles, including Secretary and Vice-President. He was a talented photographer and conducted experimental investigations into photographic processes. Throughout his life, he was involved in various scientific and technical journals, publishing articles on topics such as ancient glasses, pigments, and building materials.

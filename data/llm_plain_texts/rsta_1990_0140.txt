tldr: This article discusses the development and applications of genetically engineered reagents, made possible by recombinant DNA technology. These reagents, including enzymes, proteins, and peptides, are used for the analysis and diagnosis of analytes in body fluids, environmental analysis, and as therapeutic agents. The article also highlights advancements in enzyme engineering, such as modifying enzymes for specific activities, affinities, and stabilities, and the creation of chimeric fusion proteins for use in immunoassays.

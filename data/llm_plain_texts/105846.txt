tldr: In 1769, James Lind and Nevil Maskelyne observed a lunar eclipse at Hawkhill near Edinburgh. They recorded their findings and sent them to the Astronomer Royal. The observations helped determine the difference in meridians between Hawkhill and Greenwich. The eclipse provided valuable data for understanding the accuracy of timekeeping and observational astronomy.

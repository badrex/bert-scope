tldr: This article discusses the advancements and potential applications of nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) in studying intact biological systems. It covers a wide range of applications, from investigating molecular processes to imaging whole animals and human bodies. The author highlights the unique power of NMR spectroscopy in non-destructively investigating materials and systems and its medical potential in discriminating pathological tissue and behavior. The article also mentions the future prospects of NMR tomography in medical imaging and the need for medical physicists to learn about this field.

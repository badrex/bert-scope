tldr: Hermann Lehmann was a distinguished chemical pathologist and leading figure in the study of abnormal haemoglobins. Born in Germany, he qualified in medicine and conducted research on gastric secretion and macrocytic anaemia. Due to the Nazi regime, he moved to Basel and later worked in Heidelberg and Cambridge, where he made significant contributions to the understanding of creatine phosphate and carbohydrate metabolism. His work had a profound impact on the field of chemical pathology and medical research.

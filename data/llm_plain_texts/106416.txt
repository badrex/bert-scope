tldr: In this article, John Longfield shares his astronomical observations and the process of determining the longitude of Cork, Ireland. He discusses the instruments he used, including a transit instrument, equal altitude instrument, and astronomical clocks. Longfield also shares his experience with Smeeaton's hygrometer and its limitations. Finally, he compares his observations with those made at the Royal Observatory at Greenwich to determine the longitude of Cork.

tldr: The article describes the Quaternary sequence of an area of 120 km2 centered on Market Bosworth in Leicestershire. The entire sequence is considered the product of the Wolstonian stage. The authors discuss the environmental reconstruction, including the first ice advance, the establishment of Lake Harrison, the deposition of the Cadeby Sandur, and the readvance of ice. The study contributes to understanding the Quaternary history and stratigraphy of the English Midlands.

tldr: This article traces the historical development of theories on atmospheric circulation, starting from the early speculations of Dr. Martin Lister and Dr. Garden to the significant contributions of Edmund Halley and George Hadley. The author provides a comprehensive overview of the prevailing winds, trade winds, and west winds in different latitudes, and discusses the impact of geographical configurations on these wind patterns. The article highlights the importance of understanding atmospheric circulation for weather forecasting and climate studies.

tldr: This article discusses the evolution and current status of nickel-base superalloys, which are used primarily in gas turbines for various applications. The development of these materials has been driven by the need to improve efficiency, reliability, and operating life of gas turbines. The article highlights the key characteristics of nickel-base superalloys, their processing techniques, and the challenges faced in their development. It also touches upon the potential for further improvements and the possibility of alternative materials, such as structural intermetallics, replacing superalloys in the future.

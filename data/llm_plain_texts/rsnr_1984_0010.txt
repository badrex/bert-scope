tldr: This article discusses the transformation of the Royal Society during Thomas Henry Huxley's time as a member and its impact on British science. Huxley's election to the Royal Society in 1851 marked a turning point in the society's history, as it began to focus on professional scientists rather than patrons and well-wishers. The article highlights Huxley's contributions to the society's reform and his close association with its administrative affairs.

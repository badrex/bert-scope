tldr: 'William Hobson Mills was a prominent chemist and educator who made significant contributions to the fields of organic and stereochemistry. Born in London in 1873, Mills was educated at Spalding Grammar School and Uppingham School before attending Jesus College, Cambridge. He conducted research under T. H. Easterfield and later worked with Hans von Pechmann at the University of Tubingen. Mills held various positions at the University of Cambridge, including a personal Readership in Stereochemistry. His work and influence in the field of chemistry and education is well-documented and remembered.'

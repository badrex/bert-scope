tldr: This article discusses the Royal Society's history of acquiring and managing landed property from its establishment in 1662 to the 1930s. The Society has owned various properties, including land in Lewes, Sussex, Mablethorpe, Lincolnshire, and properties in London. The article also explains the legal challenges and decisions related to the Society's land ownership and the limitations imposed by the Charitable Trusts Acts of 1853 and 1855.

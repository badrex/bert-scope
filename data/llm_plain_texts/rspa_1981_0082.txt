tldr: This article discusses the various risks encountered in everyday living and travel in urbanized industrial societies. It analyzes the nature, magnitude, and frequency of different risks for different age groups and in relation to environmental and other factors. The author assesses the contribution of voluntary actions, such as alcohol and smoking, and evaluates the extent to which actual and perceived risk can be modified through education, engineering, and regulation.

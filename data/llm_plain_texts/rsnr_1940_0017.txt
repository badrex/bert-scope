tldr: This article discusses the development of the Royal Society's administration from its establishment in 1662 to 1860. It highlights the challenges faced by the Society in managing its finances, membership, and correspondence, and the gradual establishment of a more efficient administrative system. The article also focuses on the roles and influence of the officers and presidents of the Society, who were often responsible for promoting the interests of science within the institution.

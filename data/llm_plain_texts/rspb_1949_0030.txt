tldr: The Royal Greenwich Observatory, established in 1675, has played a significant role in the development of astronomy. Its primary focus has been on the accurate determination of the positions and motions of celestial bodies, such as the Sun, Moon, planets, and stars. The observatory has contributed to various advancements in astronomical instruments and techniques, including the discovery of stellar aberration and nutation. The author discusses the history of the observatory, its contributions, and the importance of maintaining continuity in observations for future advancements in the field.

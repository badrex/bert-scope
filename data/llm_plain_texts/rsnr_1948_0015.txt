tldr: In 1947, the Royal Society hosted a reception for thousands of chemists attending the Centenary celebrations of the Chemical Society and the Eleventh International Congress of Pure and Applied Chemistry. The event took place in Burlington House, with several other societies opening their rooms for the occasion. Exhibits showcased the contributions of British chemists and the history of the Chemical Society. Distinguished guests included ambassadors, high commissioners, and cabinet ministers.

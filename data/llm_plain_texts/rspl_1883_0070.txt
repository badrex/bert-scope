tldr: Charles Watkins Merrifield, a prominent mathematician and pioneer in naval architecture, passed away in 1884. His work focused on the application of mathematics to practical problems, particularly in naval architecture and hydraulics. Merrifield was a strong advocate for education reform, emphasizing the importance of language, drawing, and hands-on experience over traditional grammar and geometry. He contributed to various scientific organizations and held several positions, including President of the London Mathematical Society and Vice-Principal of the Royal School of Naval Architecture and Marine Engineering.

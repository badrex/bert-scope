tldr: The Joint Solar Eclipse Committee of the Royal Society, the Royal Astronomical Society, and the Solar Physics Committee conducted observations of the solar eclipse on April 16, 1893. They sent two expeditions to Senegambia and Brazil to capture photographic evidence of the event. The observations provided a large amount of material to work on, and the photographs taken at each station were considered to be of a most satisfactory character.

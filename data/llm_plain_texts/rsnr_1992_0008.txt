tldr: This article explores the various tasks and responsibilities undertaken by the Fellows of the Royal Society. It analyzes the distribution of tasks among the Fellows, their seniority, and regional patterns. The study reveals that a significant proportion of the Fellowship participates in the Society's work, and there is a notable multiplication of duties for some Fellows, facilitating cross-information and coordination of activities.

tldr: This article contains obituary notices for two fellows of the Royal Society who made significant contributions to the fields of astronomy and zoology. Robert Grant was a Regius Professor of Astronomy and Director of the Observatory in the University of Glasgow, known for his work on the history of physical astronomy and the cataloging of stars. Arthur Milnes Marshall was a zoologist who studied at Cambridge and Naples, and later became a professor of zoology at Owens College. Both men made important contributions to their respective fields and left lasting legacies in science.

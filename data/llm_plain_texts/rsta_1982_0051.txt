tldr: This article discusses recent advances in surface-specific analytical techniques, focusing on those that provide elemental or chemical analysis. The techniques are grouped based on the method of initial excitation, such as electron, photon, or ion. The author covers various techniques, including Auger electron spectroscopy, electron energy-loss spectroscopy, and appearance potential spectroscopy, and highlights their respective advantages and disadvantages. These techniques provide valuable information about the chemical and physical properties of surfaces, essential for understanding materials and their interactions.

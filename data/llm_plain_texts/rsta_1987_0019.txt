tldr: This article discusses the potential sources of lignocellulose in the European Community, such as animal manures, crop and forestry residues, domestic and industrial solid wastes, and waste paper. It also considers the policies and legislation that may affect the availability of these materials. The authors emphasize the need for economically viable routes for the utilization of these materials and highlight the importance of understanding the biology of decay processes in their treatment and conversion.

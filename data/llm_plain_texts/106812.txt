tldr: In 1793, Francis Wollaston designed a new transit circle instrument for accurately determining the right ascension and declination of celestial objects as they pass the meridian. The instrument features a fixed circle that turns in the plane of the meridian, allowing for precise measurements and improved accuracy. Wollaston's design was innovative and influential in the field of astronomy, and his work paved the way for further advancements in celestial object determination.

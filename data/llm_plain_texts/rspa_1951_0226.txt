tldr: This article provides a historical overview of East Malling Research Station and its significant contributions to fruit cultivation. The station focuses on studying the fundamental processes involved in fruit tree growth and the factors affecting their cultivation. It emphasizes the importance of rootstocks and their effects on scion varieties, as well as the close collaboration with other research institutions. The station's research has led to advancements in fruit tree growth analysis, statistical methods, and the control of tree-to-tree variation.

tldr: This article is an obituary for James Clerk Maxwell, a renowned physicist who made significant contributions to the fields of electromagnetism, kinetic theory of gases, and optics. He is best known for his development of Maxwell's Equations, which form the foundation of modern electromagnetic theory. The article highlights his life, education, and groundbreaking work, including his time at the University of Cambridge, where he held the chair of Experimental Physics and established the Cavendish Laboratory.

tldr: The article is an obituary for Herbrand Arthur Russell, the eleventh Duke of Bedford, who was a significant patron of scientific and medical research, particularly in zoology. He contributed to the growth of knowledge in mammal, bird, and fish habits, breeding, and acclimatization. The Duke was also involved in the transformation of the Zoological Society of London, establishing research stations in Woburn, and collecting a renowned private collection of living creatures. His dedication to conservation and natural history left a lasting impact.

tldr: This article discusses the changes in the Royal Society's financial management and governance from 1831 to 1938. It highlights the introduction of new financial practices, such as the annual circulation of the Society's financial statement to the Fellows, and the revision of the election process for new candidates. The article also mentions the role of J.W. Lubbock in improving the Society's financial management and the impact of some financial errors during this period.

tldr: This article discusses the zoological expeditions to the Krakatau Islands in 1984 and 1985, focusing on the invertebrate fauna that recolonized the area after the 1883 volcanic eruption. The authors explore the various methods of animal dispersal to the islands and the factors affecting the colonization process. They also highlight the diverse and unique invertebrate species found on the islands, including Coleoptera, Diptera, Hemiptera, and ants.

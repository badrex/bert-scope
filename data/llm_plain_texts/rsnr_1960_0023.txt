Moray was a soldier, statesman, scientist, and counselor to kings, who played a significant role in establishing the Society and promoting the new experimental philosophy of his time. His dedication and enthusiasm for science made him a valuable asset to the Society, and his connections with the highest circles of power allowed him to advance its interests. Moray's life and work serve as a testament to the importance of science and its impact on society during the early modern period.'

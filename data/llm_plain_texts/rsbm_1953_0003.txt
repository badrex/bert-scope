tldr: 'William Thomas Calman was a renowned zoologist and systematic biologist who specialized in the study of crustaceans and rotifers. Born in Dundee, Scotland, Calman's early interest in science was nurtured by his mother and mentors at his school. He later joined the Dundee Working Men's Field Club and Dundee Naturalists' Society, where he met influential zoologist D'Arcy Wentworth Thompson. Calman's career included teaching and research positions at various institutions, and he authored several influential books and papers in the field of zoology. His contributions to the understanding of animal classification and evolution have left a lasting impact on the scientific community.'

tldr: This article discusses the structural unity of the reconstructed North Atlantic continent and its geological provinces. The author proposes that these provinces are the disrupted fragments of one continuous continent and that they have an essential structural unity. The article also examines the concept of chelogenic cycles and their relation to the continental drift and the development of major structural provinces.

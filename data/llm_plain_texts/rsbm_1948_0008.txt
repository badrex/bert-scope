tldr: Arthur Robert Hinks was a British astronomer and geographer who made significant contributions to the fields of astronomy and geography. He worked on the determination of the solar parallax and the mass of the Moon using observations of the minor planet Eros. Hinks also made advancements in the accuracy of meridian observations and the development of the Sheepshanks Telescope. His work in cartography and map projections helped bridge the gap between astronomy and geography.

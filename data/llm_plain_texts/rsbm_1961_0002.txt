tldr: 'Percy George Hamnall Boswell was a dedicated geologist and educator who made significant contributions to the understanding of Eocene and Pliocene deposits in East Anglia. He also played a crucial role in the development of geology as a discipline at Imperial College and the University of Liverpool, where he served as a professor. His research on detrital minerals and glacial deposits helped shape the field of geology and had important economic implications during and after World War I.'

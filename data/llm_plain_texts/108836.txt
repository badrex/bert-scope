tldr: The author, Warren de la Rue, describes his observations and photographic experiments during the total solar eclipse of July 18th, 1860, at Rivabellosa, Spain. He discusses the challenges and preparations for capturing images of the eclipse using the Kew photo-heliograph and an achromatic telescope. The author also shares his experiences with the expedition organized by the Astronomer Royal and the construction of a dedicated photographic observatory for the event.

tldr: The authors studied two strains of Trypanosome diseases in Nyasaland, affecting domestic animals such as cattle, horses, goats, sheep, pigs, and dogs. They found that the Mvera Cattle strain and the Wild-game strain have different characteristics and effects on various animals. The authors also discussed the role of tsetse flies in spreading the diseases and the methods used to study the trypanosomes' morphology and distribution.

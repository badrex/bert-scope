tldr: Errol Ivor White was a renowned palaeontologist and Keeper of Palaeontology at the British Museum (Natural History). He dedicated over sixty years to researching and studying various groups of fossil fishes, particularly those found in the Old Red Sandstone and late Silurian strata. White's work contributed to expanding the Museum's collections and improving the display of its fossil mammal gallery. He was also involved in the creation of an adjunct to the Museum building to accommodate the growing collections and staff.

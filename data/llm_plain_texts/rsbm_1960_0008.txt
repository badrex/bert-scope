tldr: Claude Dixon Gibb was an Australian engineer, business man, and administrator who made significant contributions to the fields of engineering and war production. He worked for C. A. Parsons, where he improved organization and procedure, and held various positions in the British government during World War II. Gibb was responsible for the development of the 'Centurion' tank and played a crucial role in the production of weapons and instruments. After the war, he returned to Parsons as Chairman and Managing Director, overseeing the expansion and modernization of the company's facilities.

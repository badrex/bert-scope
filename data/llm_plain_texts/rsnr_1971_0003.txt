tldr: Jean André De Luc was a Swiss natural philosopher and scientist who made significant contributions to the fields of atmospheric science, mountain climbing, and thermometer and hygrometer development. He was elected as a Fellow of the Royal Society in 1773 and later served as a Reader to Queen Charlotte. De Luc's work on measuring the heights of mountains and understanding the nature of the atmosphere helped advance scientific knowledge during his time.

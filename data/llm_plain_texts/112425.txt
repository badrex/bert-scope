tldr: This article discusses the remains of a large extinct Lama species, Palauchenia magna, found in Quaternary deposits in the Valley of Mexico. The author compares the fossils with other cameloid quadrupeds and concludes that this species had closer affinities to the Lamas and Vicugnas. The author also refers to other related species and describes the dental and osteological differences between them.

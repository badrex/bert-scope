tldr: This article is a biography of William Lawrence Balls, a botanist and textile technologist who made significant contributions to the fields of plant physiology, genetics, and agriculture. Born in 1882, Balls was introduced to science and craftsmanship from a young age by his parents. He attended King Edward Middle School and later Cambridge University, where he studied botany and earned a First in Part One of the Tripos. After completing his studies, Balls worked as a cryptogamic botanist in Egypt, where he conducted research on plant genetics and physiology. He later became a botanist in the Department of Agriculture, where he continued his research and made important discoveries in plant pathology and breeding.

tldr: The author, Monsieur Le Cat, provides a detailed account of the malignant fevers that raged in Rouen from 1753 to 1754. He describes the symptoms, progression, and treatment methods used during that time, including bloodletting, emollient clysters, and paregorics. The author also discusses the connection between the dry weather and the increase in these diseases.

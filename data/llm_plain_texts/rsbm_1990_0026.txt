tldr: The article is a biographical memoir of Robert Courrier, a founder of reproductive endocrinology. It highlights his early life, education, and experiences during World War I. The memoir also discusses his significant contributions to the understanding of the endocrine glands, particularly the ovary, testis, and thyroid, and his influential mentor, Pol Bouin. Courrier's work laid the foundation for modern knowledge in reproductive physiology.

tldr: This article provides a historical overview of the study of lichens, a group of plants that grow on trees, walls, rocks, and stones. It discusses the work of various botanists, including Micheli, Haller, Linnaeus, and Dillenius, who have classified and described different species of lichens. The article also highlights the various uses of lichens in medicine and economics, such as their use in treating lung diseases and as a source of dyes and tannins. The author emphasizes the importance of further research to better understand the taxonomy and uses of lichens.

tldr: This article explores the scientific correspondence between John Aubrey and John Lydall during the period of Commonwealth Oxford. It highlights the scientific interests of the Oxford group, their collaboration, and the influence of Trinity College on their work. The correspondence provides insights into the range of scientific subjects that interested the Oxford virtuosi in the early 1650s, including mechanics, mathematics, and empirical studies. The article also discusses the connections between the Oxford group and the founding of the Royal Society.

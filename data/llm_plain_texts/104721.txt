tldr: In a letter to the President, Daniel Peter Layard describes his experience and observations of an earthquake that shook London on March 8, 1749. He reports that the earthquake was felt throughout the city, particularly in Hampstead, and was stronger than the one on February 8. Layard also mentions that two smaller shocks preceded the main event.

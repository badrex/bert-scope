tldr: 'Nevil Vincent Sidgwick was a prominent figure in the world of science and education. Born into a gifted family, he was encouraged to pursue his love of science and natural history. Sidgwick attended Rugby School and later Christ Church, Oxford, where he excelled in both classics and natural sciences. He worked in several laboratories, including Ostwald's in Leipzig, and conducted research on organic compounds and physical chemistry. Sidgwick was elected to a fellowship at Lincoln College, Oxford, where he spent the rest of his life. His contributions to science and education left a lasting impact on the field.'

tldr: The article provides a detailed account of the life and scientific achievements of Marcello Malpighi, a prominent Italian physician and scientist. Malpighi made significant contributions to the fields of anatomy, botany, zoology, microscopy, and physiology. His work involved studying the structure and function of various organs, including the lungs, spleen, and testicles, as well as the anatomy of plants and animals. Malpighi was also involved in several disputes with other scientists and published numerous treatises and observations throughout his career. He was elected as a member of the Royal Society in 1669 and continued his research until his death.

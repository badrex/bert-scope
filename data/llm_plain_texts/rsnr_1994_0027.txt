It discusses the challenges faced by science professors and students during this period, the contributions of notable scientists, and the development of various scientific disciplines in the university. The article highlights the slow recovery of science after World War I, the introduction of the D.Phil. degree, and the growth of physics and chemistry departments. It also mentions the influence of European scientists on Oxford's research and the importance of mathematics and astronomy in the university's history.

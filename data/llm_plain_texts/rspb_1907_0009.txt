tldr: In his Anniversary Address, Lord Rayleigh discusses the scientific achievements and challenges faced by the Royal Society. He highlights the loss of distinguished fellows and foreign members, including Ludwig Boltzmann and Harry Marshall Ward, who contributed significantly to various fields of science. He also mentions the progress made in combating diseases like Sleeping Sickness and Malta Fever. Furthermore, he talks about the Royal Society Catalogue of Scientific Papers, the National Physical Laboratory, and the ongoing research and development in various fields.

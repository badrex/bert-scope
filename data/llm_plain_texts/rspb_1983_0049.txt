tldr: The study examines the ovigerous fraena of barnacles, specifically Conchoderma auritum, and their role in the attachment of egg masses. It also investigates the presence or absence of ovigerous fraena in various barnacles and their relationship to branchiae. The research reveals the specialized structure and function of ovigerous fraena and setae, as well as the differences in egg mass attachment and development between lepadomorph and balanomorph barnacles.

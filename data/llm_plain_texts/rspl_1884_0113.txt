tldr: The Royal Society held its annual meeting in June 1885 to elect new fellows. The meeting included the nomination of scrutators, the collection of votes, and the admission of new members. The Bakerian Lecture on the Sun's corona was delivered by William Huggins, but its publication was deferred.

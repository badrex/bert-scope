tldr: This article explores the similarities between perceptions and scientific hypotheses, suggesting that the methods of acquiring scientific knowledge can serve as a working paradigm for understanding perception. The author argues that perceptions, like scientific hypotheses, allow for the recognition of familiar situations or objects from limited data and can be influenced by contextual knowledge. The article also discusses the importance of understanding signal codes and stored knowledge in order to better understand perceptual processes.

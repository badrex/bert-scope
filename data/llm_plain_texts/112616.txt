tldr: This article discusses the third year of the Magnetic Survey of the Southern Ocean conducted by Sir James Clark Ross and Captain Francis Rawdon Crozier between 1839 and 1843. The author, Edward Sabine, provides a detailed exposition of the operations and observations made during this period, including the Southern Atlantic between Cape Horn and the Cape of Good Hope. The article also mentions the author's plans to connect and coordinate the three portions of the survey in a future memoir.

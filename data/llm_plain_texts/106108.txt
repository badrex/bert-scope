tldr: In January 1768, Glasgow experienced unusually cold weather. Alexander Wilson, a professor of astronomy, documented the temperature using a thermometer and observed the freezing of water and ice evaporation. He also noted the effects of the cold on the atmosphere and the surface of various materials. This detailed account provides valuable insights into the understanding of weather patterns and the impact of extreme cold on the environment.

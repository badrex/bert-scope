tldr: The author investigates the hemolytic action of sodium glycocholate and its interaction with serum albumin. They find that sodium glycocholate is a weak hemolytic agent, but when mixed with serum albumin, it rapidly causes hemolysis. This discovery sheds light on the controversies regarding hemolysis and hemoglobinuria in jaundice.

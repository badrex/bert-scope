tldr: The author reviews the functions of the nervous system and its relation to other vital functions. They divide nerves into two classes: those that convey information to the brain and spinal cord and those that connect to vital organs. The author explains that the ganglionic nerves, which connect to vital organs, support processes of secretion and assimilation. They also discuss the independence of the nervous and muscular powers from the sensorial power and the importance of respiration in the functioning of these powers.

tldr: The author, Paul Dudley, shares his observations on the growth and productivity of various plants, fruits, and trees in New England. He discusses the success of English plants in the region, the size and yield of fruit trees, and the remarkable growth of the Buttonwood and Locust trees. He also explains the phenomenon of color mixing in cornfields and shares an unusual case of an apple tree that bears fruit without blooming.

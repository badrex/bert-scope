tldr: This article discusses the quantification of risks in major disasters, focusing on societal risks rather than individual risks. It presents different measures of harm, including personal risks and financial measures, and examines the factors contributing to risks, such as knowledge and its transmission. The author also explores the timescales of disasters and how they affect risk assessment, with a particular focus on nuclear risks and long-term large-scale pollution risks.

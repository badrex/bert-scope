tldr: 'The article describes the development, life cycle, ultrastructure, and phylogenetic position of Pasteuria ramosa, an obligate endoparasite of Daphnia magna. The parasite has a polymorphic life cycle and is transmitted through waterborne spores. The 16S rDNA sequence analysis shows that Pasteuria ramosa belongs to the low G+C Gram-positive branch of eubacteria. This study helps clarify the confused taxonomic history of the parasite and confirms its classification as a bacterium.'

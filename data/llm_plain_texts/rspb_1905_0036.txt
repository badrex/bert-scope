tldr: The author describes a new species of Cephalodiscus, C. nigrescens, found in the Antarctic Ocean. The colony is massive and has transparent, opalescent branches with polypides dwelling in tubes. The polypides have pigmented cells and various body features, including plumes and a pointed caecum. The species is distinguished from C. dodecalophus by its massiveness, blackness, and large size.

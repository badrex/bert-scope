tldr: This article provides a detailed eyewitness account of Mount Etna's eruption in 1755, as reported by the magistrates of the city of Mascali in Sicily. The eruption included a shower of stones and black sand, a river of water that scalded those who touched it, and streams of fire that threatened the surrounding areas. The magistrates express their fear and terror in the face of this natural disaster.

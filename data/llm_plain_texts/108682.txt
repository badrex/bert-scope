tldr: The author describes the discovery of an extinct genus of the Sturionidae family, Chondrosteus, found in the Lias Formation at Lyme Regis. The study of this genus, which differs from present-day Sturgeons, confirms its classification within the same family. The article provides a detailed description of the anatomical features and the differences in the structure, form, and position of the natatory organs of Chondrosteus compared to modern Sturgeons.

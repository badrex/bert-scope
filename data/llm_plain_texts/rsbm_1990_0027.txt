tldr: John Douglas Eshelby was a prominent figure in solid state physics, known for his work on dislocations and internal stresses in solids. He made significant contributions to the understanding of these phenomena through theoretical calculations and collaborations with other scientists. Eshelby also served in the Royal Air Force during World War II, working on radar technology before returning to academia and earning his Ph.D. in physics.

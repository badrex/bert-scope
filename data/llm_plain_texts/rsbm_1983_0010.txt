tldr: 'The article is a biographical memoir of Ludwig Guttmann, a pioneering neurosurgeon who made significant contributions to the field of neurosurgery. Born in 1899 in Upper Silesia, Guttmann trained under the renowned neurosurgeon Otfrid Foerster and established a neurosurgical unit in Hamburg. He later became a neurologist and neurosurgeon at the Jewish Hospital in Breslau, where he faced challenges under the Nazi regime. Despite the adversity, Guttmann continued his work and eventually emigrated to Portugal. His story highlights his dedication to medicine and his resilience in the face of adversity.'

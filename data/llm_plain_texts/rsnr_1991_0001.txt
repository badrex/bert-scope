tldr: This article investigates the identity of Jaques Du Moulin, a Fellow of the Royal Society in 1667. It explores the confusion surrounding his identity, as he was not part of the well-known Du Moulin family of French origin. The author presents evidence that Jaques Du Moulin was a James Miln or Milne from Aberdeen, who lived in the south of France and was likely not medically qualified. The article also discusses the context of the Royal Society and the scientific community during this time.

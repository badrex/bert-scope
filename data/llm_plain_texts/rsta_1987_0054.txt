tldr: This article discusses the influence of aerospace-performance requirements on the development of advanced structural materials in the 1990s. It highlights the interdisciplinary approach between aircraft designers and materials scientists, and the need for engineered materials with tailored properties. The article also presents a mass-saving evaluation methodology, and explores the development of advanced materials such as Al-Li alloys, magnesium alloys, metal matrix composites, and high-temperature aluminium alloys for aerospace applications.

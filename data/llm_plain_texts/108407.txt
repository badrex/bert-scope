tldr: The author investigates the decomposition of rubian, a substance found in madder, and its role in the formation of color in madder-based dyes. They discover that rubian is not a colorant itself but plays a crucial role in the transformation of other substances into color-producing compounds. The author also discusses the challenges in isolating rubian in its pure form and the methods used to study its properties and decomposition products.

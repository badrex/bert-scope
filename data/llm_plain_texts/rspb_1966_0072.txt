tldr: The authors applied numerical taxonomy to study the morphological, physiological, pathological, and cultural characters of 23 different isolates of Helminthosporium, including 19 belonging to the genus Drechslera. They used an electronic computer to calculate similarity coefficients and construct similarity matrices and dendrograms. The study confirmed the separation of Helminthosporium, Drechslera, Bipolaris, and Stemphylium as distinct genera. The authors found that H. triseptatum, H. dematoideum, and H. biformae form a separate group, while H. avenae, H. siccans, and H. avenaceum appear to be conspecific.

tldr: Alexander Scott was a renowned inorganic chemist and educator who made significant contributions to the field of chemistry. Born in Selkirk, Scotland, he studied at Edinburgh and Cambridge Universities before becoming a prominent figure in the scientific community. Scott worked on determining accurate atomic weights and methods for preparing high-purity substances. He also played a crucial role in developing scientific teaching in the North of England and was involved in the formation of the Association of British Chemical Manufacturers. Scott was a fellow of the Royal Society and served as the president of the Chemical Society.

tldr: This article examines the glacial erratics and interglacial deposits found at Hoxne, Suffolk. The author identifies various rocks and their origins, suggesting that the erratics came from northern Britain. The study also confirms the position of Hoxne in the Gipping glacial phase and the interglacial deposits between the Lowestoft and Gipping glacial phases.

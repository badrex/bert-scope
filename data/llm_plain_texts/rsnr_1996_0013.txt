tldr: This article is a review of Richard Nichols' book, "The Diaries of Robert Hooke, The Leonardo of London, 1635-1703." The book explores Hooke's life, his work as a scientist, and his relationships with colleagues and contemporaries. It covers Hooke's education, his role as Curator of the Royal Society, his inventions, and his contributions to various fields such as architecture, meteorology, and blood transfusion. The review also discusses Hooke's relationships with other famous figures like Newton, Aubrey, Petty, and Wren.

tldr: The author describes the geological, botanical, and zoological collections made on Rodriguez Island during the Transit of Venus Expeditions in 1874-75. The island consists of doleritic and basaltic rocks, primarily Olivine-rich, with various textures and mineral compositions. The author discusses the different rock formations, their localities, and the unique features of these rocks, such as nodular and variolitic textures.

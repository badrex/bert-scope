tldr: This article explores the life and work of John MacCulloch, a versatile scientist of the 19th century, who made significant contributions to geology and chemistry. MacCulloch taught at the East India Company's Military Seminary at Addiscombe, where he influenced the development of geological research. His work on Scottish geology and his role as a chemist for the Board of Ordnance are highlighted, along with his election as President of the Geological Society of London.

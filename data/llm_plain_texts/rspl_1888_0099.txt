tldr: This article presents the observations and photographic results of the total solar eclipse that occurred on August 29, 1886. The authors discuss the accuracy required in adjusting an equatorial for photographic purposes during the eclipse and share their findings from the expedition, including descriptions of the eclipse and the corona, as well as the determination of the photometric intensity of the coronal light.

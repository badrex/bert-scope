tldr: The author describes well-preserved Belemnite fossils found in the Oxford clay at Christian Malford, Wilts. The fossils reveal the soft parts of the Belemnite, allowing for a detailed analysis of its anatomy, including the structure of its shell, mantle, and muscular fibers. The author also discusses the evolutionary relationships of Belemnites, which possess characteristics from various modern cephalopod genera.

tldr: The President of the Royal Society, Lord Florey, honors six distinguished scientists for their significant contributions to various fields. A. L. Hodgkin is recognized for his groundbreaking work in neurophysiology, revealing the ionic mechanism of the nerve impulse. R. A. Lyttleton is celebrated for his theoretical studies in astronomy and geophysics, influencing problems in galaxy formation, the solar system, and more. J. C. Kendrew is commended for his molecular structure research on sperm-whale myoglobin, paving the way for molecular biology. H. C. Husband is recognized for his engineering work on large structures like radio telescopes. H. W. Thompson is honored for his spectroscopy applications in chemistry, and D. H. Wilkinson is celebrated for his experimental nuclear physics research.

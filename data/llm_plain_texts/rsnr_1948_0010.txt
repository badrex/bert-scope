tldr: This article celebrates the retirement of Mr. H. W. Robinson, the Librarian of the Royal Society of London. Robinson's career spans 46 years, during which he has been instrumental in cataloging and preserving the Society's extensive library and archives. He has also contributed significantly to the study of the history of science, particularly through his work on Robert Hooke's diary and his involvement in the founding of the Annals of Science and the British Society for the History of Science. Despite retiring, Robinson will continue to assist in editing the Newton Letters.

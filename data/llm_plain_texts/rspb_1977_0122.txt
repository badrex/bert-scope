tldr: The article discusses the global smallpox eradication program initiated by the World Health Organization in 1967. The program successfully eliminated smallpox by identifying and containing the last known cases in 1976. Key factors in the program's success include effective reporting-surveillance mechanisms, participation of local health workers and village populations, and the production of improved vaccines. The article highlights the importance of international collaboration and the favorable epidemiological characteristics of smallpox that facilitated its eradication.

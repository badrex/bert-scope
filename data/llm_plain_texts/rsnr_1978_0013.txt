tldr: John Ellis was an influential naturalist in the 18th century who made significant contributions to the fields of zoology and botany. He studied zoophytes, plant preservation, and the natural history of coffee, among other topics. His work was highly regarded by his contemporaries, including Carl Linnaeus, and he played a crucial role in connecting the Royal Society with the North American colonies.

tldr: 'The authors conducted experiments using the hot wire anemometer to study the effect of muscular contraction on blood flow in skeletal muscles. They found that during muscle contraction, there is an initial increase in blood flow, followed by a rapid decrease and a significant increase after the contraction. The authors suggest that this is due to a dual mechanism involving reflex actions and the accumulation of vasodilator substances in the muscle fibers.'

tldr: Mr. Halley observed the lunar eclipse in Chester on October 19, 1697, and shared his findings with the Royal Society. He noticed that the Moon's shape changed during the eclipse, which helped him understand the relationship between the Moon and the Earth. This observation contributed to the study of celestial mechanics and the solar system.

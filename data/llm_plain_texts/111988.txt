tldr: The author, A.W. Hofmann, investigates the properties and chemical composition of chrysaniline, a yellow coloring matter derived from coal-tar. Chrysaniline is a triamine that forms various salts, such as nitrates, hydrochlorates, and bromides. The author also discusses the similarities and differences between chrysaniline, rosaniline, and leucaniline, and the potential for transforming these substances.

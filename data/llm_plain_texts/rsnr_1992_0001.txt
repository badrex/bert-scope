tldr: The article explores the life and work of John Wilkins, a key figure in the establishment of the Royal Society of London and the promotion of natural religion. Wilkins, a scholar and statesman, was a supporter of the Protestant Reformation and played a significant role in the development of early modern science. His work encompassed various fields, including defending the Copernican theory, promoting experimental science, and developing a universal language. Wilkins's ideas and projects contributed to the transformation of European cosmology and laid the foundation for the Newtonian world system.

tldr: The study investigates the host selection and oviposition behavior of Microplectron fuscipennis, a parasitic wasp that targets specific species of sawflies. The author discusses the factors that influence the female wasp's ability to choose the correct host for its offspring's development, including shape, size, and physico-chemical properties of the host. The research also explores the controversy surrounding the role of instinct and tropic responses in host selection.

tldr: M. Pingre, a member of the Royal Academy of Sciences in Paris, shares his observations of Venus and its satellites. He describes his use of telescopes, including an achromatic telescope made by Mr. Dollond, to study the planet and its satellites. He also discusses the latitude and longitude of his observatory and the challenges of observing the eclipses of the satellites. Pingre's observations contribute to the understanding of planetary science and the technology of telescopes.

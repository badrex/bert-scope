tldr: 'The article discusses the life and work of William Dobinson Halliburton, a key figure in the development of biochemistry in Britain. Halliburton, a Professor of Physiology at King's College London, contributed to the field through his research on heat fractionation and chemical description of tissues. His work helped shape the discipline of biochemistry, which emerged within departments of physiology. Halliburton's focus on the intersection of medicine, physiology, and biochemistry made him a pioneer in the field.'

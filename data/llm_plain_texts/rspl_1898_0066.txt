tldr: This article is an obituary for Thomas Jeffery Parker, a prominent figure in the field of zoology and biology education. Parker was a student of Huxley and played a significant role in the development of the Huxleyan method of laboratory instruction. He contributed to various scientific journals, authored several books, and served as a professor of biology at the University of Otago in New Zealand. Parker was also a skilled museum curator, known for his work on preserving cartilaginous skeletons and contributing to the Otago Museum's collections.

tldr: This article provides a detailed history of Nos. 6, 7, 8, and 9 Carlton House Terrace, tracing their development and the notable occupants who lived there from 1829 to 1967. The Royal Society moved into the buildings in 1967, and the article discusses the various changes and controversies surrounding the property over the years. The Terrace's original design by architect John Nash and its significance in British history are also highlighted.

tldr: 'John Kenyon Netherton Jones was a prominent organic chemist known for his work in carbohydrate research and contributions to the war effort during World War II. He studied at Waverley Grammar School and Birmingham University, where he earned a B.Sc. with first-class honors. Jones worked with Edmund Hirst and made significant contributions to the synthesis of ascorbic acid. He also worked on explosives research during the war. Jones held various positions at Bristol University, Manchester University, and Queen's University, Kingston, Ontario, where he was appointed the Chown Research Chair of Chemistry in 1953.'

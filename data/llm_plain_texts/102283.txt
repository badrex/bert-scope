tldr: This article presents the case of Hannah Taylor, a six-year-old child with an unusually large body size, who died due to complications related to inflammation and kidney issues. The author, Dr. Hen. Sampson, provides a detailed account of the dissection and analysis of her body, highlighting the large size of her internal organs and the presence of inflamed and rotten lungs. The suddenness of her death is attributed to the impact of inflammation on the nerves responsible for respiration.

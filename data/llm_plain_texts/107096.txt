tldr: The author provides a historical and anatomical description of a unique amphibious animal called Proteus anguinus, found in a lake in Carniolia. Despite its resemblance to some lizard larvae, the author argues that it is a complete animal. The animal is eyeless, has a slow and deliberate movement, and produces a hissing sound. The author also describes the animal's internal and external anatomy, including its small, black eyes and branchial appendages.

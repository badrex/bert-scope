tldr: This biographical memoir recounts the life of William Dickson Lang, a prominent geologist and natural historian who served as the Keeper of the Department of Geology at the British Museum (Natural History) from 1928 to 1938. Lang's passion for natural history and geology began at a young age, and he went on to make significant contributions to the fields, particularly in the study of butterflies and insects. The memoir highlights Lang's personal life, family background, and academic journey, as well as his impact on the world of science.

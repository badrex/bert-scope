tldr: The article is a biographical memoir of David Meredith Seares Watson, a renowned geologist and palaeontologist. Born in 1886, Watson made significant contributions to the study of fossil reptiles and the Karroo System in South Africa. He worked in various institutions, including the University of Manchester, the British Museum (Natural History), and University College London, where he held positions as an honorary lecturer and professor. Watson's research and travels led to the refinement of the subdivision of the Beaufort Series and the establishment of meaningful biostratigraphical zones.

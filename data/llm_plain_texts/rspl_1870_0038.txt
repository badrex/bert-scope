tldr: The author examines the structure and organization of the Calamites, a type of plant found in coal measures. They describe the plant's anatomy, including its central pith, woody zone, and bark, and classify it as an independent order, distinct from the Equisetaceae family. The author also discusses the various modifications and adaptations found in different Calamites species and their significance in understanding the plant's evolution and relationship to other plants.

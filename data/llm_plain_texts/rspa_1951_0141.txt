tldr: This article discusses the early developments in biophysics research at King's College, University of London, focusing on the integration of physical, biological, and other disciplines to advance biological knowledge. The author highlights the importance of microscopy and microspectrometry techniques in studying cells, particularly through tissue culture. The research covers a wide range of biological materials, including amphibian, animal, and plant tissues, as well as spermatozoa, protozoa, and embryonic and adult muscle and bone.

tldr: James Irvine Orme Masson was a prominent Australian chemist and educator who made significant contributions to the field of chemistry. He studied at the University of Melbourne and later worked with renowned scientists like Sir James Walker and Sir William Ramsay. Masson's research focused on solubility, ionic reactions, and the radioactive springs of Bath. He also served in the Royal Naval Volunteer Reserve during World War I and contributed to the development of efficient methods for manufacturing cordite. Masson held various positions in academia, including Vice-Chancellor of the University of Sheffield, and was a key figure in the development of Australian science.

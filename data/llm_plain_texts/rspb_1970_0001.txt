tldr: In this Anniversary Address, Lord Blackett highlights the accomplishments of various scientists who received awards from the Royal Society in 1969. The recipients include Sir Peter Medawar for his work on immunology, Dr. G. E. R. Deacon for his contributions to physical oceanography, Dr. F. Sanger for his research on RNA sequences, Professor C. W. Oatley for his development of the scanning electron microscope, Dr. F. S. Dainton for his studies on chemical reactions, and others. The address also discusses concerns about the British Ph.D. system and its impact on the technological development of British industry.

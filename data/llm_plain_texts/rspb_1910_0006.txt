tldr: The author conducted experiments with the venom of the Causus rhombeatus snake and observed its effects on chickens. The venom caused local ecchymosis, drowsiness, muscular weakness, and respiratory issues. Post-mortem examinations revealed superficial hemorrhages in the viscera, especially the heart and lungs. The author also compared the effects of the venom with those of stovaine and cocaine on isolated muscles.

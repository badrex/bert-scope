tldr: The article is a memoir of Colonel Sir Proby Cautley, an engineer and palaeontologist who played a significant role in the construction of the Ganges Canal, one of the largest irrigation canals in the world. Cautley was also responsible for making, describing, and classifying a vast collection of sub-Himalayan fossils, which he presented to the British Museum. His contributions earned him a Fellowship in the Royal Society and a knighthood for his services to India.

tldr: This article explores the lives and works of Edward Gibbon, author of the six-volume 'Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire', and George Grote, author of the 12-volume 'History of Greece'. Both were Fellows of the Royal Society and shared similarities in their backgrounds and accomplishments. Gibbon came from a family with business and political connections, while Grote overcame a humble background and a lack of formal education to become a prominent historian and university administrator. The article highlights their contributions to literature, science, and education during the 18th and 19th centuries.

tldr: Edward Frankland Armstrong was a prominent chemist and scientific leader in the fields of biochemistry, enzyme chemistry, and industrial chemistry. He made significant contributions to the understanding of glucosides, enzymes, plant oxidases, plant pigments, and hormones. Armstrong also played a crucial role in the development of heterogeneous catalysis and the large-scale production of acetic acid and acetone during World War I. Throughout his career, he held various positions in the chemical industry and was involved in numerous scientific organizations, including the British Association for the Advancement of Science, the Society of Chemical Industry, and the British Standards Institution.

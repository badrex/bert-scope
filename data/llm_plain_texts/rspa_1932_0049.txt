tldr: This article pays tribute to C. T. Heycock, a pioneer in inorganic chemistry, and Sir Howard Grubb, a renowned astronomer and optical instrument maker. Heycock's work on alloys and metallurgy, along with his teaching and contributions to the University Chemical Laboratories, is celebrated. Grubb's dedication to constructing and improving large telescopes and optical instruments for observatories around the world is also recognized. Both individuals are remembered for their significant contributions to their respective fields.

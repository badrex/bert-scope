tldr: The author investigates the ancient English system of weights and measures, focusing on the period before Henry VII's reign. They analyze historical statutes and conduct experiments to determine the standards of the old pound and gallon, concluding that the old pound was lighter than the present Troy pound and the gallon contained 224 cubic inches. The author also discusses the differences between the old and new standards, including the introduction of the Troy pound and the change in the bushel measure.
